Facts and Figures: Sexual Abuse in India

Overall Trends

A comprehensive 2024 study highlights an unsettling rise in sexual offences in India, with highest crime rates reported in 2021.

Andaman and Nicobar Islands recorded the highest rates of sexual offences.

Gender Disparity

Girls are disproportionately affected, accounting for the majority of reported cases.

Nearly 49.3% of urban Indian college students reported childhood sexual abuse.

Male Abuse

51% of men reported experiencing sexual harassment before the age of 18.

A significant proportion were victimized at age 10 or younger.

Comprehensive Analysis

Sexual abuse remains one of the gravest violations of human rights, affecting individuals across all age groups, genders, and societal strata. Recent studies have shed light on alarming trends and patterns that demand immediate attention and action.

Public Space Harassment

An exploratory study in Lucknow reveals the pervasive nature of harassment on public transport. Female students reported frequent victimization, often in plain sight of other passengers, who fail to intervene due to normalized settings.

Child Sexual Abuse

A 2022 study found that nearly 49.3% of urban Indian college students experienced sexual abuse during childhood. The majority of victims were girls under 12, with offenders predominantly male. Alarmingly, a significant proportion of these offences were committed by family members.
