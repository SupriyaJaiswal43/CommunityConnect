Project Kawach
Our first project, Project Kawach, focuses on child sexual abuse, with a commitment to educating approximately 50,000 children through awareness and prevention programs. We aim to empower children, parents, caregivers, and communities by helping them recognize signs of child sexual abuse, understand boundaries, and adopt safe behaviors. This includes teaching self-defense techniques, helping children differentiate between safe and unsafe touch, encouraging them to assertively refuse inappropriate advances, and building a clear understanding of consent. We address the various forms of abuse, including physical, emotional, verbal, and online exploitation, to ensure a comprehensive approach to prevention. Additionally, we advocate for creating safer environments for children by implementing measures such as thorough background checks for individuals working with children, establishing reliable reporting mechanisms, and strengthening community-driven vigilance and support systems. To further support our mission, we provide legal assistance and mental health support to survivors, ensuring they have access to the resources and care they need for recovery and justice.
Our Approach
Providing comprehensive support services for victims and survivors, including counseling, legal advocacy, and access to medical care.
Working closely with stakeholders, including law enforcement, healthcare professionals, and educators, to advocate for stronger protections and legislation against child sexual abuse.
Empowering communities to take action against child sexual abuse through training, resources, and partnerships with local organizations.
Conducting ongoing research and evaluation to improve our strategies and ensure they are effective in preventing and responding to child sexual abuse.
